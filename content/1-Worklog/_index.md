**On this page**, you will need to introduce **how** your worklog is presented. **How many weeks** did you complete the program in? **What did you do** during those weeks?

Typically, and as a standard practice, a worklog is carried out over a period of about 3 months (throughout the internship), with the weekly content as follows:

**Week 1:** [Getting familiar with AWS and its core services](1.1-week1/)

**Week 2:** [Practicing basic AWS labs (IAM, S3, EC2, VPC)](1.2-week2/)

**Week 3:** [Continuing to practice advanced AWS labs (ELB, Auto Scaling)](1.3-week3/)

**Week 4:** [In-depth study of EC2, S3, IAM and practicing resource management](1.4-week4/)

**Week 5:** [Reviewing, consolidating knowledge, and completing outstanding labs](1.5-week5/)

**Week 6:** [Further study of EC2, S3, IAM and practicing deployment and resource management](1.6-week6/)

**Week 7:** [Practicing creating, configuring, managing EC2, and studying data storage with S3](1.7-week7/)

**Week 8:** [Researching and designing the Serverless system architecture for the Electronic Receipt (Preview Bill) project](1.8-week8/)

**Week 9:** [Developing Lambda, integrating DynamoDB/S3, and configuring API Gateway (REST & WebSocket)](1.9-week9/)

**Week 10:** [Optimizing AWS resource costs, writing technical blog posts, and documenting the Serverless system](1.10-week10/)

**Week 11:** [Completing features, integration testing, and bug fixing for the Electronic Receipt system](1.11-week11/)

**Week 12:** [Official deployment, finalizing documentation, and project summary demo](1.12-week12/)
